# Examine Route File: Descriptive

## What This Stage Protects
- Reporting-frame honesty, denominator interpretation, measurement scope, and subgroup boundary fidelity.
- Counts, rates, distributions, cross-tabs, coverage, and reporting-window integrity as the descriptive support surface.
- Clear separation between description and explanation.

## What This Stage Prohibits
- Driver, effect, predictive, or mechanistic language.
- Treating correlation, co-movement, or subgroup separation as explanation.
- Generic pairplots, broad association sweeps, or unsupervised structure hunting by habit.
- Inventing new subgroup taxonomies, unsupported weighting, unsupported standardization, or smoothing choices that belong in `analyze`.

## What This Stage Defers
- Exact headline summaries, subgroup granularity, weighting or standardization formulas, binning, aggregation conventions, visualization grammar, and exact fragility procedures.
- Any summary-package decision that belongs to `analyze`.

## What Triggers Backtracking In This Stage
- The reporting frame or denominator basis cannot be reconstructed from visible artifacts.
- Hidden filtering, pre-aggregation, or cleaning consequences make "what is present" unclear.
- Reasonable approved subgroup or aggregation choices materially change the headline description.
- Descriptive support is too weak to sustain the approved claim boundary.
- The work starts needing explanatory, inferential, predictive, or causal language.
